HOW I GOT HERE

  • The Bronx High School of Science

    September 2010 - June 2014

    One of the nine specialized high schools in the city of New York. It requires a score passing the second highest threshold (after Stuyvesant) on the Specialized High Schools Admissions Test (SHSAT).

  • Steven T. Gee, P.C.

    June 2010 - May 2011

    At the ripe age of 14, I took my first job at this law firm after applying for the Summer Youth Employment Program (SYEP). After the program was scheduled to end, I discussed the possibility of staying with Steven and fortunately for me, he was grateful to have me stay on until the next summer. I began working after school rather than joining clubs as many of my classmates did.

  • Asian Americans For Equality (AAFE)

    June 2011 - April 2012

    The summer before my second year of high school, I found myself working for a non-profit organization that specialized in low-income housing for Asian Americans. I was an assistant book keeper and assisted with payroll, data entry, and filing. I found this job through fostering relationships within the Asian American community in Chinatown, Manhattan. Unfortunately, AAFE’s office closed at 6pm which meant after my hour commute from my high school, I would only be able to work 2 hours a day.

  • Leader's Summer Day Camp

    June 2012 - August 2012

    My third summer in high school was spent working as a camp counselor at a day camp in Queens. I helped students in the third grade with reading comprehension, math problems, and arts and crafts. I was also responsible for overseeing their recess and lunch time. I found that I loved to teach and to share knowledge.

  • Kumon

    October 2012 - May 2013

    In my third year of high school, I wanted to join clubs and have more flexibility with my schedule after school. So, I searched for opportunities near my high school in the Bronx and luckily, I found Kumon and it was only a 15 minute walk from my school. A few friends and I applied and started working as tutors for third to fifth graders. We had a flexible schedule which meant I was able to finally join a few clubs. I chose Key Club and The Urban Dance Club, which proved to be great opportunities to give back to society, and to socialize and connect with school mates.

  • Gigino Trattoria and Take Away

    June 2013-January 2016

    The summer before my last year in college, I started working at an Italian restaurant in TriBeCa. Although I started out as an assistant bookkeeper, I eventually transitioned into hostess, delivery manager, and then assistant manager due to the needs of the restaurant. I would continue to work here for the entirety of my senior year of high school and during some breaks (summer 2015, winter 2015) from college. It’s crazy for me to look back on this year and realize that I attended school from 8am-3pm and then I would work from 4pm-9pm, getting home sometimes at 10pm and I did this usually 4-5 days a week, sometimes on the weekends instead of weekdays.

  • The State University of New York at Buffalo

    August 2014-May 2019

    The largest public university in New York. I chose this school to distance myself a little from a chaotic home life. I majored in Psychology, with a special interest in the social sciences and anthropology.

  • Maloney & Porcelli

    December 2014-January 2016

    The winter of my first year in college, we had a break for a month and a half. Naturally, I went and found a job. Luckily, at this point, technology helped with everything. I utilized a hospitality focused job platform called Harri.com and found a job at a high-end steakhouse in Midtown, next to the Palace Hotel. I created lasting relationships with my fellow hostesses and supervisors, and I was asked to come back for the next summer.
    So, in the summer of 2015, the summer before my sophomore year of college, I began working two jobs: hostessing at Maloney & Porcelli and being the assistant manager for Gigino Trattoria. I continued to work at both locations in the winter of 2015 as well.

  • John Dory Oyster Bar

    May 2016 - August 2016

    The summer before my junior year of college, I found a job as the Maitre’D for The John Dory Oyster Bar. This was a completely new environment for me because I had never handled the door alone at such a busy spot. I learned to have thick skin and how to diffuse tense situations with customers.

  • MAMO

    May 2017 - August 2017

    The summer before my senior year of college, I found a job at MAMO, a cute French Riveria inspired restaurant in SoHo. The owners had another restaurants— a celebrity favorite in Antibes called Mama Michelangelo. The NYC location had quite a few celebrity diners as well, such as Justin Timberlake, Leonardo DiCaprio, Jennifer Lopez, Alex Rodriguez, and Nick Jonas. JT was my favorite, he came in more than once and treated the staff like we were his friends! I spent the whole summer training and testing other hostesses (after being trained myself) to prepare for my imminent departure in late August, because they were quite picky with staff and rightfully so! MAMO was probably my absolute favorite restaurant to work at despite my short tenure. It wasn’t only because I was able to meet celebrities, I was also able to sit instead of stand for 6 hours! But more importantly, they really went above and beyond to provide exceptional customer service and every one of the staff members were truly fantastic.

  • Nickel & Diner

    June 2017 - August 2017

    A few weeks into working at MAMO, I decided I had the time and energy for a second job (partly due to my ability to sit instead of stand at MAMO, which is unheard of in the hostessing/hospitality world). I started working as a breakfast host at Nickel & Diner. It was only a few blocks away from MAMO and the two schedules never overlapped. It was a new trendy and casual dining spot at the time so it was always busy—but I love busy.

  • Cosme

    December 2017-August 2018

    I wonder if Cosme needs any introduction… I’ll give one anyways. Cosme is an exceptional contemporary Mexican restaurant in Flatiron. They are currently ranked #23 in The World’s 50 Best Restaurants 2019. Cosme was by far the most high end restaurant with the largest team I’d ever worked with. I started working here in the winter of my senior year of college and again the following summer. Winter is a very busy season for all restaurants, especially so for Cosme. We sometimes had 5 girls at the host stand and the sheer volume of us sometimes startled and intimidated guests. ‘You outnumber us!’ -party of 4.

  • SUGARFISH by sushi nozawa

    December 2018 - July 2019

    While completing my bachelors degree with a final online course, I started working at SUGARFISH by sushi nozawa, a very popular sushi restaurant originating from California. I started out at the Flatiron location and moved to the SoHo location when during it’s grand opening. I was the sole hostess and helped the SoHo team build out a foundational 8-person host team before being promoted to Server. Sugarfish is a very different type of restaurant when compared to the others where I’ve worked. Their whole motto is efficiency, it applies to the back of house, front of house, management—everything. In SoHo, we would often find ourselves short-staffed because it wasn’t a very stable location. We would go the first few hours with just a few customers to having 20 parties on the wait list. It was at here that I learned just how crucial teamwork, collaboration, and morale is to keeping a team running. If one team member slacked, the weight was felt on everyone else. Here, I also trained dozens of people and again learned how much I loved to share knowledge and guide people through unfamiliar processes.

  • SixPlus

    August 2019 - Present

    After 6 years in hospitality gig industry, I finally began my search for a stable, full-time job. I scoured Indeed and AngelList for hospitality-tech companies and I was fortunate enough to have my first interview with an amazing company founded by amazing people—SixPlus. Have you ever called a restaurant to ask about having a large party or private event in their space? Did they take down your contact info and say someone will reach out but you never heard from them again? SixPlus aims to eliminate this outdated and inefficient process by creating a marketplace where clients can browse available venues and then directly connect with the managers in charge of said venue.
    Since SixPlus is an early stage startup, I wear a lot of hats. Although my title is Customer Relations & Office Admin, my duties range from on boarding clients, troubleshooting bugs, managing customer relationships, experiential marketing, sales, and business development.

  • COVID-19 and Web Development

    March 2020 - Present

    SixPlus, along with the rest of the hospitality industry (and many other industries), was hit hard by the COVID-19 pandemic. The whole team, with the exception of the software developers and the co-founders, was furloughed. I took this opportunity to teach myself something that always interested me, web development and programming. From March until now, I’ve learned HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and jQuery. Next up is Node JS. I’m planning to continue learning more languages and useful programs while I work full-time. I love spending my time productively, so by default, I love learning :). Please don’t hesitate to reach out if you have any opportunities, questions, or comments! I love hearing feedback.
